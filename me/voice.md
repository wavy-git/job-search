## Communication preferences

- **Direct.** Low tolerance for filler, buzzwords, cheesy or performative phrasing.
- **Assertive, leader-like.** Professional writing reads like a leader wrote it, not a job-seeker.
- **Factual over narrative.** Concrete facts and outcomes carry more weight than adjectives.
- **No melodrama.** No "I'm thrilled to," "passionate about," "deeply committed to."
- **No apologetic or deferential framing.** No "I might be wrong but," "if it's not too much trouble," "I hate to ask."
- **Don't ship mediocre.** If a draft isn't exceptional, name what's missing and fix it before showing.
- **Voice dictation context:** Maz often uses voice dictation; transcription artifacts (extra words, missing punctuation, homophones) appear in source material. Don't pattern-match those into voice rules.

### Punctuation
- **Em dashes:** use sparingly. Acceptable when they meaningfully aid clarity (e.g., a tight parallel structure). Never as a narrative flourish; reads AI-generated.
- **En dashes:** avoid entirely.

### Words and phrases to never use
- Buzzwords: "synergize," "leverage" (as a verb), "streamline," "circle back," "low-hanging fruit," "move the needle," "boil the ocean," "best-in-class," "world-class," "rockstar," "ninja," "thought leader."
- Melodrama openers: "I'm thrilled," "excited," "honored," "humbled," "passionate."
- Consultant-speak: "operational picture," "strategic product call," and similar.
- Any phrase that signals performance over substance.

## Tone by channel

Different channels call for different registers. The rules above apply everywhere; these tune the register.

### Resume (narrative zones — summary, scope lines, bullets)
- Tight prose, hard numbers, demonstration over labeling.
- No keyword stuffing.
- Every word earns its place.

### Resume (skim zones — Core Competencies, Tools, Certifications)
- Keyword surface acceptable. Recruiter Boolean and older ATS parsers scan these.

### Cold recruiter email
- Brief fit summary upfront (1-2 sentences of where Maz lands against the role).
- Direct ask for a call.
- Sample exemplar: the Forsyth Barnes email to Jessica Dobie — concise, fit-anchored, asks for the call.

### LinkedIn invite note (≤300 chars)
- Brief intro + fit hint + "glad to connect either way."
- Sample exemplar: the Brian Feeley (Liberty Personnel) connect note.

### LinkedIn warm DM to a hiring manager
- Friend-to-friend tone, not pitch.
- Reference something they've publicly shared (post, video, talk) — shows actual attention.
- Ask for **perspective or advice**, not a meeting.
- Surface builder identity honestly ("still build apps on the side, can't help it").
- Mention applying without making the message about the application.
- Sample exemplars:
  - The Matthew Wensing (Customer.io) message responding to his launch video and the "people make systems" line.
  - The Leanna Miller (Nava) message — short, casual, no name-drops, asks for advice.

### Interview answers
- Direct, factual, leader-altitude.
- Don't apologize for the path (VP → TPM transition was deliberate; own it).
- Use breadth as evidence, not as defense.
- Specific stories carry more weight than principles.
