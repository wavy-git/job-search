# Outreach

Workflow + formats + tone guidance + exemplars for producing outreach artifacts per application — DMs, cold emails, LinkedIn invites, follow-ups, referral hunts.

## Three-touch model

Every score-4-or-5 application gets THREE parallel touches:

1. **ATS submission.** The tailored resume goes through the company's application system. This is the system-of-record touch.
2. **HM or founder LinkedIn DM.** Direct message to the hiring manager (or founder, at Series A). Warm tone, not pitchy. Asks for perspective on the team they're building.
3. **Referral hunt.** Find a 1st or 2nd degree LinkedIn connection at the company who can refer or warm-intro.

Cold-apply-only at Director level converts at 2-5%. Three-touch is the path to 20-40%.

Document each touch in the outreach pipeline file as it happens.

## Cold recruiter email format

Used when: a role is recruiter-fronted (Forsyth Barnes, Wayoh, Liberty Personnel, Ladders, RemoteHunter) and the recruiter's email is known.

Structure:
1. Subject: "<Role title> — Maz Mavvaj resume attached" (specific, scannable).
2. Brief fit summary (1-2 sentences): Maz's strongest matching pillars for this role.
3. Direct ask for a call.
4. Resume attached.

**Exemplar — Forsyth Barnes (Jessica Dobie), Sr/Principal Forward Deployed PM:**

```
Subject: Sr/Principal Forward Deployed PM — Maz Mavvaj resume attached

Hi Jessica,

I applied for the Sr/Principal Forward Deployed PM role. Forward-
deployed since my Infotech dev days, now own a $1.4B utility platform
at NG. Prior Comcast (Log4Shell crisis analytics), prior Infotech VP
(10K → 120K merchants, 30+ FI national card network). Still build
apps on the side, can't help it.

Resume attached. Happy to hop on a call.

Thanks,
Maz
```

**Exemplar — Liberty Personnel (Brian Patrick Feeley), Director PM:**

```
Subject: Director Product Management — Maz Mavvaj resume attached

Hi Brian,

20+ yrs in product, 9+ at leadership level. 12 direct reports + 50+
employees + 700+ field technicians at Infotech VP. Now 30-person
cross-functional team on $1.4B regulated infrastructure program at
NG. Marlton NJ, MBA Widener.

Resume attached. Happy to talk.

Thanks,
Maz
```

## LinkedIn invite note format (≤300 chars)

Used when: connecting with a recruiter, HM, peer, or referral target.

Structure:
1. Brief intro (one line: "Hi [name], applied for [role] / following your work / etc.").
2. Fit hint (one line: strongest matching credential).
3. "Glad to connect either way."

**Exemplar — Brian Patrick Feeley invite:**
```
Hi Brian — applied to your Director PM role for Greater Philadelphia.
20+ yrs in product (Head of Products at NG, VP at Infotech, MBA
Widener). Local to Marlton NJ. Sent resume to bf@libertyjobs.com
directly. Glad to connect either way given you place across IT roles
in the area.
```

## Warm DM to HM / founder (LinkedIn)

Used when: HM is identifiable, accessible via LinkedIn, and has public content (videos, posts, talks) Maz can reference authentically.

Structure:
1. Open by referencing something the person publicly shared (post, video, talk) — proves Maz actually paid attention.
2. Make a personal connection if there's one (shared mentor, shared experience type, shared philosophy).
3. Mention applying — but don't make the message about the application.
4. Ask for **perspective or advice on the team they're building**, not for a meeting.
5. Surface builder identity honestly if it fits ("still build apps on the side, can't help it").
6. Close warm, not transactional.

**Exemplar — Matthew Wensing (Customer.io VP Product & Design):**
```
Watched your video, and the way you held up your team, and the line
about 'people make systems,' brought back someone I once worked for
who led the same way. He's been gone a few years now. I think of him
when someone gets this part of leadership right. Thanks for putting
that out. Hope we get to talk sometime.

Also, I'm thinking about applying for the Director role too. If you
ever have a few minutes, would love your honest take on what you're
looking for and the team you're trying to build. Either way, glad
there are still people leading like this.
```

**Exemplar — Leanna Miller (Nava Director Product, recent hire):**
```
Hey Leanna,

Just saw you joined Nava a couple months ago, that's awesome. I
applied for the Director of Product role and figured I'd say hi.
20+ years building and turning around products inside large,
regulated systems. Still build things on the side, can't help it.
Any advice would mean a lot.

Thanks,
Maz
```

## Follow-up cadence + tone

### After LinkedIn connection accept
- 5-7 days post-accept: send a follow-up DM if no prior message exchanged. Reference the role applied to. Keep warm.

### After cold recruiter email
- 5-7 business days: light follow-up. Confirm receipt, ask if any next steps.

### After warm DM to HM
- Don't follow up unless there's a clear new reason (e.g., they posted something else worth referencing). Pestering kills the warm tone.

### After ATS submission with no response
- 1-3 weeks: typical recruiter response window. After 3 weeks, consider the cold-apply leg of the touch as silent; rely on warm-path legs.

## HM identification procedure

For each score-4-or-5 application:

1. Read the JD for "Reports to" or "You'll work with" language.
2. Search LinkedIn for the company + the parent function (e.g., "VP Product Customer.io" for a Director Product role).
3. Cross-check: hire date (recent hires might be the HM themselves, peer Director, or recently-joined-still-onboarding).
4. Build a brief on the HM (background, recent public content, mutual connections). Save to research dossier (location TBD — see pending.md).
5. If no clear HM identifiable, drop the warm-DM leg of three-touch and double down on referral hunt + cold email.

---

## Referral hunt procedure

For each score-4-or-5 application:

1. Search LinkedIn for 1st-degree connections at the company.
2. If none, search 2nd-degree connections (mutual contacts who could intro).
3. Prioritize: connections at the team being applied to > connections at the company in adjacent functions > general company contacts.
4. Reach out to the strongest connection asking for a warm intro to the HM (or for context on the team).
5. Log the touch in the outreach pipeline file.

---

## Outreach logging

For every touch (DM, email, invite, referral ask), log a row in the outreach pipeline file with:
- Date
- Company
- Application URL (joins to applications pipeline file)
- Target person + their role
- Channel (linkedin-connection / linkedin-message / email / referral-ask / phone / in-person)
- Message summary (1-2 sentences capturing the gist + tone)
- Status (drafted / sent / accepted / responded / no-response / declined)
